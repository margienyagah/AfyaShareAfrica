THE CHALLENGE

Healthcare emergencies shouldn't begin with fundraising.

Families often start from zero.

When illness strikes, many households rely on WhatsApp appeals, church announcements, or emergency contributions to pay hospital bills. Treatment may be delayed while funds are raised.

Communities are willing to help.

Across Kenya, people regularly support one another through churches, chamas, SACCOs, and extended families. The willingness already exists.

The missing piece is preparedness.

Healthcare financing is often reactive rather than planned. AfyaShare explores how existing community structures can prepare for healthcare before crisis happens.

OUR INSIGHT

Communities already know how to care for one another.

Through conversations with chama members, churches and community leaders, one pattern consistently emerged: people willingly support members during illness, but healthcare financing remains largely unstructured.

Many groups have well-established systems for bereavement support, savings and development projects, yet healthcare often depends on urgent appeals after someone becomes sick.

AfyaShare is exploring whether those same trusted community structures can become a sustainable foundation for healthcare preparedness.

HOW AFYASHARE WORKS

Preparing for healthcare starts long before someone gets sick.

AfyaShare builds on community trust by helping groups prepare together rather than fundraise during emergencies.

1

Create a Community Wallet

A church, chama, SACCO, employer or extended family establishes a shared health wallet with transparent governance agreed by members.

2

Contribute Gradually

Members make flexible contributions over time, building a dedicated healthcare fund before emergencies occur.

3

Access Care Quickly

When a member requires treatment, funds can be accessed without relying on urgent fundraising or delayed community appeals.

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Common Questions

Is AfyaShare an insurance company?

No. AfyaShare is developing a community-powered healthcare preparedness model that complements existing healthcare financing systems.

Can AfyaShare replace insurance?

No. Our goal is to improve access to healthcare by helping communities prepare together. Future integration with insurance providers is an important area we intend to explore.

Is AfyaShare already operating?

AfyaShare is currently preparing its first pilot through community research, stakeholder engagement and partnership development.
